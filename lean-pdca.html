Lean Concepts & Applications ‚Äî Checkout Delays

Ran a Lean time study of a campus convenience store checkout process, identified the primary bottleneck, and executed a PDCA pilot with practical countermeasures to reduce overrides and waiting time.

Pilot results
  • Overrides dropped from 12% ‚Üí 8% (33% reduction)
  • Payment cycle time improved from 0.6 ‚Üí 0.5 min
  • Average lead time improved from 5.5 ‚Üí 5.1 min
Study setup
  • Two sessions of ~35‚Äì40 min during rush hours
  • 60 customers observed, 2 registers
  • Captured queue length, override types, approvals, cycle time
Primary bottleneck
  • Payment step driven by overrides/mismatches and rework

Problem

Checkout delays during peak times created long queues and inconsistent throughput, primarily due to payment overrides and rework.

What I measured

  • Lead time per customer
  • Override types + frequency
  • Manager approval waits
  • Queue length over time

Countermeasures (PDCA pilot)

  • Standard work: ‚ÄúScan, Bag, Pay‚Äù
  • 6S: improved cash-wrap layout
  • Express lane: 5-item lane during peak
  • Error-proofing: amount-match check card + hotkeys

Lean wastes addressed

  • Waiting: approvals/overrides
  • Motion: layout inefficiencies
  • Rework: mismatch correction
  • Variation: inconsistent cashier flow

How I‚Äôd scale it

  • Expand pilot duration and add more time windows
  • Track weekly override rate + lead time dashboard
  • Train new staff with a short standard-work guide

Why it matters

  • Shows execution: observe ‚Üí diagnose ‚Üí pilot ‚Üí report results
